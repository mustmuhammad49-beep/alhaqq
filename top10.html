TOP 10 ATTACKS AND ANSWERS

The ten you will hear most. One answer each. Straight from the Book.

Save this page to your home screen. Carry it into the comments.

ATTACK 01

Islam was spread by the sword — Muslims forced people to convert

THE ANSWER

2:256 says there is no compulsion in religion. You cannot force belief into a heart, Allah judges what is inside it. Christians and Jews lived under Muslim rule for over a thousand years and stayed Christian and Jewish. If the sword was the method it did a terrible job.

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:256
ATTACK 02

Muslims worship Muhammad — Islam is no different from prophet worship

THE ANSWER

Open Surah 80. Allah rebukes Muhammad for turning away from a blind man. Nobody rebukes their own god. The Quran calls him a messenger over and over and commands worship of Allah alone. Veneration is not worship and the Quran draws that line itself.

Surah Ali 'Imran 3:144
ATTACK 03

What is the Quran? Recitation, the Speech of Allah, a Book, and Oral Transmission

THE ANSWER

The Quran does not describe itself as only one kind of thing. It describes itself four ways at the same time: as a recitation said out loud, as the actual speech of God, as a written Book, and as a text carried in human memory word for word.

Surah Al-Hijr 15:9
ATTACK 04

Islam borrowed everything from Judaism and Christianity — it's not an original revelation

THE ANSWER

One God sent one message through every prophet, so of course the message matches. The Quran says it openly in 42:13, He ordained for you the same religion He gave Noah, Abraham, Moses and Jesus. Consistency from the same Author. Plagiarism requires a different author. You are describing confirmation.

Surah Hud 11:13
ATTACK 05

Rejecting Hadith means rejecting Islam itself — you cannot be Muslim without Hadith

THE ANSWER

6:114 asks, shall I seek other than Allah as a judge when He is the one who sent down the Book fully detailed? The Quran calls itself complete, detailed and fully explained. If it needed a second book those verses would be false. I take the Quran's word over the compilers.

Surah Al-An'am 6:38
ATTACK 06

The Quran has no prayer times — you need Hadith to know when to pray

THE ANSWER

The Quran gives the times itself. 11:114 establishes prayer at the two ends of the day and the approach of night. 17:78 runs from the sun's decline to the darkness of night plus the dawn recitation. 24:58 names dawn and isha by name. The claim dies the moment you actually read.

Surah Al-Isra 17:78
ATTACK 07

Muhammad married a child — Islam condones child marriage

THE ANSWER

The Quran sets the standard in 4:6, marriageable age plus sound judgement before any transfer of responsibility. A child satisfies neither condition. Whatever historical traditions say, I hold the Quran above them. The Book's own standard rules out the interpretation you are pushing.

Surah An-Nisa 4:6
ATTACK 08

The Quran says Allah prays — doesn't that prove Islam is incoherent?

THE ANSWER

This objection reveals a method — and once the method is exposed, the entire category of these arguments collapses. Context and actor determine meaning: Arabic, like English, contains words that carry multiple meanings depending entirely on who is performing the action and in what context.

Surah Ali Imran 3:7
ATTACK 09

The Quran makes a scientific mistake about embryology — bones don't form before flesh

THE ANSWER

Surah Al-Mu'minun 23:14 describes human embryonic development in a precise sequence: a drop (nutfah), then a clinging substance (alaqah), then a chewed-like lump (mudghah), then bones (izam), then the bones clothed with flesh (lahm). The Quran was revealed in the 7th century — more than a thousand years before the microscope was invented in 1677, before any human being could observe an embryo at these stages.

Surah Al-Mu'minun 23:14
ATTACK 10

The Bible is the perfectly preserved, internally consistent Word of God, while the Quran has contradictions

THE ANSWER

Christian apologists love to hold up 4:82, where the Quran stakes its own claim: "Then do they not reflect upon the Quran? If it had been from other than Allah, they would have found within it much contradiction." Good — that is a fair test, and a falsifiable one.

Surah An-Nisa 4:82
